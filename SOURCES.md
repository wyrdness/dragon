# Sources & References

## Ancient & Classical Sources

### Mesopotamian
- *Enuma Elish* (c. 1800 BCE). Babylonian creation epic featuring Tiamat, primordial chaos dragon.
- *Epic of Gilgamesh* (c. 2100 BCE). References to dragon-like beasts.

### Greek & Roman
- Homer. (c. 800 BCE). *Iliad* and *Odyssey*. Early dragon/serpent references.
- Hesiod. (c. 700 BCE). *Theogony*. Python and other draconic beings.
- Apollonius of Rhodes. (3rd century BCE). *Argonautica*. Dragon guarding the Golden Fleece.
- Ovid. (8 CE). *Metamorphoses*. Various dragon myths including Cadmus.
- Pliny the Elder. (77 CE). *Natural History*. Descriptions of dragons and serpents.

### Chinese Classical Texts
- *I Ching* (Book of Changes, c. 1000 BCE). Dragon symbolism.
- *Shan Hai Jing* (Classic of Mountains and Seas, c. 4th century BCE). Dragon descriptions.
- *Huainanzi* (c. 139 BCE). Dragon mythology and symbolism.

### Medieval European
- *Beowulf* (c. 1000 CE). Anglo-Saxon epic with dragon as final adversary.
- *Völsunga saga* (13th century). Norse saga featuring Fafnir the dragon.
- *Historia Brittonum* by Nennius (9th century). Welsh dragon legend (Vortigern's tower).
- *The Golden Legend* by Jacobus de Voragine (1260). St. George and the Dragon.
